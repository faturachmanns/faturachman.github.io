Social Media & Mental Health Balance - Exploratory Data Analysis

This project is an exploratory data analysis based on 500 users to study how daily screen time, sleep quality, stress level, exercise habits, and happiness are connected. The main goal of this analysis is to understand how digital behavior and lifestyle choices influence mental well-being in everyday life. The dataset includes demographic information, social media usage patterns, sleep quality scores, stress levels, physical activity frequency, and happiness index.

Key insights: The analysis shows that screen time is the most influential factor in mental well-being. Higher daily screen time is strongly linked to poorer sleep quality, higher stress levels, and lower happiness. Sleep quality plays a central role in emotional health, where users with better sleep consistently report higher happiness and lower stress. Stress and happiness move in opposite directions, meaning that when stress increases, happiness tends to decrease. Exercise helps reduce stress especially when done regularly (around 4–6 times per week), but its effect is not purely linear. Meanwhile, age and days without social media show very little influence compared to daily habits.

Recommendations: Users are encouraged to reduce excessive screen time, especially before bedtime, to protect sleep quality. Improving sleep routines through consistent bedtimes and limiting late-night scrolling can directly support better mental health. Regular physical activity should be maintained as a practical way to manage stress. Overall, this study suggests that focusing on daily habits such as screen use, sleep, and activity level is far more effective for improving happiness and mental well-being than relying only on demographic factors.
