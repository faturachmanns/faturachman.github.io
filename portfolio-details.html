GameSphere – Global Video Game Sales Analytics (1980–2017)

Executive Summary – Key Insights

In today’s highly competitive and rapidly evolving video game industry, publishers and developers face crucial questions: Which genres should be prioritized? Which platforms offer the best opportunities? And which regions deliver the highest potential?

Through the GameSphere Analytics project, these questions were addressed using historical sales data, market analysis, and ARIMA-based forecasting models.

Key Findings:

  • Priority Regions: North America & Western Europe — the highest average sales globally.
  • Top Genres: Action, Sports, and Shooter — representing the largest market share.
  • Leading Platforms: PlayStation 2, Xbox 360, and PlayStation 3 — strong distribution networks and user bases.
  • Dominant Publishers: Nintendo, Electronic Arts, and Activision — each excelling within specific genre segments.

Executive Summary – Business Strategy Direction

Recommended Strategic Focus:

  1. Regional Focus: Prioritize launches in markets with high purchasing power and rapid consumer response, such as North America and Western Europe.
  2. Genre Allocation:
    • Action (40%) → steady and growing
    • Sports/Shooter (30%) → large but stagnant
    • Puzzle/Strategy (20%) → for niche market exploration
  3. Platform & Publisher Strategy: Expand digital distribution via PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X, and form partnerships with EA (Sports) and Activision (Shooter).
  4. Adaptive Monitoring: Utilize daily Tableau dashboards and conduct weekly reviews; pivot strategies rapidly if KPI deviation exceeds 20%.

The GameSphere Analytics project illustrates how data-driven forecasting can guide strategic decision-making in the gaming industry — from identifying profitable regions and genres to optimizing partnerships and digital distribution strategies for sustainable growth.
